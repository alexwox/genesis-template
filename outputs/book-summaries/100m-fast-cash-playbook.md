## Tactical Ideas
### Use scarcity, urgency, and high touch together
- **ID:** `100m-fast-cash-playbook-003`
- **Problem(s) Solved:**
  - Helps premium promotions feel valuable and time-bound instead of like random discount blasts.
- **How It Works:** The playbook emphasizes that fast cash offers work because they are scarce, urgent, and high-touch. Scarcity caps spots, urgency compresses decision time, and high-touch delivery justifies the big-ticket price.
- **Usage Example(s):**
  - An agency could offer only 12 conversion-audit intensives for the month, close enrollment within five days, and deliver them personally to justify premium pricing.
- **Source Notes:** Sections: How Fast Cash Plays Work; Important Points
- **Related Items:** None

### Push-to-consult sequence
- **ID:** `100m-fast-cash-playbook-004`
- **Problem(s) Solved:**
  - Helps businesses monetize warm lists when the premium offer still benefits from conversation and qualification.
- **How It Works:** For more complex or expensive offers, the campaign should move the buyer into a consult flow where the team can qualify, build belief, and close the premium package live. This works best when the promise is high value and the audience already has trust.
- **Usage Example(s):**
  - A coaching brand could email its customer base about a limited advanced mastermind and route interested buyers into short sales calls rather than sending them straight to checkout.
- **Source Notes:** Sections: Push To Consult Sequence
- **Related Items:** None

### Push-to-checkout sequence
- **ID:** `100m-fast-cash-playbook-005`
- **Problem(s) Solved:**
  - Helps businesses collect cash faster when the offer is simple enough to buy without a salesperson.
- **How It Works:** For offers with clear value and low buying friction, the playbook recommends routing buyers straight to automated checkout. This removes scheduling lag and lets warm traffic convert immediately.
- **Usage Example(s):**
  - A supplement brand could launch a limited VIP bundle to existing buyers with direct links in email and SMS, collecting revenue the same day instead of booking calls.
- **Source Notes:** Sections: Push To Automated Checkout Sequence; Sample Email And Text Sequence

## Frameworks
### Fast cash plays as quarterly warm-audience promotions
- **ID:** `100m-fast-cash-playbook-001`
- **Problem(s) Solved:**
  - Helps owners generate cash quickly without waiting on new customer acquisition.
- **How It Works:** Fast cash plays are limited-time, high-ticket promotions run to current customers, past customers, and engaged leads who already know the business. They are designed to create a short burst of cash flow on top of the core offer.
- **Usage Example(s):**
  - A services business with a slow quarter could run a premium implementation sprint to former buyers and active prospects rather than trying to spin up cold traffic from zero.
- **Source Notes:** Sections: What Fast Cash Plays Are; How Fast Cash Plays Work
- **Related Items:** None

### 10x the 10% rule
- **ID:** `100m-fast-cash-playbook-002`
- **Problem(s) Solved:**
  - Helps businesses see how a small slice of customers buying a much higher-priced offer can dramatically increase revenue and profit.
- **How It Works:** If 10% of customers buy a 10x-priced premium offer, revenue can double with far less acquisition strain because the audience is already paid for. The playbook uses this as the mental model for why premium back-end offers matter.
- **Usage Example(s):**
  - A gym with 300 members could sell a premium transformation intensive to 30 members instead of trying to double its membership count immediately.
- **Source Notes:** Sections: Fast Cash Plays - What/How/Why
- **Related Items:** None
