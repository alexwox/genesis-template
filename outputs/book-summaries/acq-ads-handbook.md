# ACQ Ads Handbook

## Executive Snapshot
This handbook has two high-value layers. First, it introduces the `Ad Kaleidoscope`, a system for milking winners rather than constantly starting from zero. Second, it catalogs twenty repeatable ad frameworks drawn from multiple businesses, showing that the same persuasion patterns can be ported across B2B, B2C, products, services, and launches.

## Tactical Ideas
### Stop Killing Winners Because You Are Bored
The Henry Ford story at the start makes the core operational point: internal repetition is not market repetition. A winning ad should run until performance drops, not until the team is tired of looking at it.

### Remix Winners Before Refilming
The fastest path to more ad volume is post-production variation: speed, filters, backgrounds, fonts, headlines, medium, format, swapped meat, and effects. These remixes buy time while bigger remakes are being produced.

### Remake Winners In The Real World
When a winner starts tiring, keep the winning script and change real-world variables like props, examples, setting, talent, or multiple variables together. This preserves what made the original work while extending shelf life.

## Strategic Ideas
### Put 80 Percent Of Effort Into Winner-Based Variations
The handbook explicitly applies the Pareto principle to ads: most results come from a minority of winners, so most creative effort should go into multiplying those winners. New ideas still matter, but they should occupy the smaller experimental slot.

### Frameworks Travel Better Than Scripts
Hormozi's point is not to copy the exact example ad. It is to extract the structure, then swap in your own avatar, pain, mechanism, proof, and CTA. That is why the same frameworks can work across Gym Launch, ALAN, Skool, and a book launch.

### Use Different Frameworks For Different Buying Temperatures
The named frameworks map to different jobs: origin stories for anti-objection proof, educational whiteboard ads for complex offers, bribes and contests for aggressive action, and long-term result ads for higher-quality buyers.

## Frameworks
### Ad Kaleidoscope
The sequence is: identify winners, remix them, remake them, and spend a smaller slice of time on brand-new ideas. When a new winner appears, run it through the same process again.

### Origin Story / Anti-Proof Framework
Make an outrageous promise, then stack evidence that conditions were bad or unfavorable so the result feels even more impressive. This works because it destroys the viewer's built-in reasons why the result would not work for them.

### Specific Avatar / Application Framework
Call out a narrow avatar, name their current metric, contrast it with a bigger target state, and offer a gated next step. This attracts higher-fit prospects and filters out casual traffic.

### Bribe / Prize Framework
Give away something unusually valuable, attach it to a short time window, show a clear path to winning, explain the support participants get, and make losing still valuable by bundling free tools or training.
