## Tactical Ideas
### Attraction offers lower friction without killing demand
- **ID:** `100m-money-models-how-to-make-money-003`
- **Problem(s) Solved:**
  - Helps businesses create first-step offers that bring new buyers in cheaply and quickly.
- **How It Works:** The attraction section includes models like win-your-money-back, giveaways, decoys, buy-X-get-Y, pay-less-now-or-pay-more-later, and free goodwill. These are meant to get people into motion, not maximize profit on day one.
- **Usage Example(s):**
  - A med spa could use a low-friction paid consult that is credited back on purchase, creating movement while keeping the buyer economically anchored.
- **Source Notes:** Sections: Section II Attraction Offers; Win Your Money Back; Giveaways; Decoy Offer; Buy X Get Y Free; Pay Less Now or Pay More Later; Free Goodwill Offer
- **Related Items:** None

### Upsells should capture the obvious next purchase
- **ID:** `100m-money-models-how-to-make-money-004`
- **Problem(s) Solved:**
  - Helps businesses increase customer value without forcing customers into irrelevant products.
- **How It Works:** The book includes classic, menu, anchor, and rollover upsells, all designed to raise spend once the buyer already said yes. The best upsell feels like the natural next step after the initial decision.
- **Usage Example(s):**
  - After buying a basic training program, a customer could be offered either a done-with-you upgrade, a higher-touch version, or a premium option anchored against the base package.
- **Source Notes:** Sections: Section III Upsell Offers; The Classic Upsell; Menu Upsell; Anchor Upsell; Rollover Upsell
- **Related Items:** None

### Downsells monetize the no without forcing a bad-fit sale
- **ID:** `100m-money-models-how-to-make-money-005`
- **Problem(s) Solved:**
  - Helps businesses keep revenue from prospects who want help but cannot accept the main offer as-is.
- **How It Works:** Payment-plan downsells, trial-with-penalty structures, and feature-reduced versions let the business keep a portion of buyers who would otherwise walk away completely. Downselling is a value-preserving fallback, not an excuse to weaken the main offer upfront.
- **Usage Example(s):**
  - A software company can offer a lighter package with fewer seats or a paid trial instead of letting a budget-constrained prospect disappear.
- **Source Notes:** Sections: Section IV Downsell Offers; Payment Plan Downsells; Trial With Penalty; Feature Downsells
- **Related Items:** None

## Strategic Ideas
### Continuity should reward staying, not just repeat billing
- **ID:** `100m-money-models-how-to-make-money-006`
- **Problem(s) Solved:**
  - Helps businesses design recurring revenue that feels valuable enough to keep rather than easy to churn.
- **How It Works:** The continuity section uses bonus offers, discount offers, and waived-fee structures to make ongoing payment feel like a good deal. Continuity works best when it adds ongoing reason to remain, not merely a subscription line item.
- **Usage Example(s):**
  - A membership business could reward annual commitment with exclusive bonuses, better economics, and removed setup fees instead of simply charging monthly forever.
- **Source Notes:** Sections: Section V Continuity Offers; Continuity Bonus Offers; Continuity Discount Offers; Waived Fee Offer
- **Related Items:** None

### Solve the customer's next problem, not the founder's favorite idea
- **ID:** `100m-money-models-how-to-make-money-007`
- **Problem(s) Solved:**
  - Helps businesses choose backend offers that buyers will actually take.
- **How It Works:** The early story of the book shows that the winning backend offer was not the founders' passion project but the thing customers most obviously wanted next. Money models work when they follow customer logic, not founder novelty.
- **Usage Example(s):**
  - A language-learning business may be better off upselling conversation practice or certification prep than a totally unrelated mastermind the founder finds more exciting.

### The whole model matters more than any isolated offer
- **ID:** `100m-money-models-how-to-make-money-008`
- **Problem(s) Solved:**
  - Helps teams optimize the full customer economics instead of arguing over whether one single offer is perfect.
- **How It Works:** The book's final emphasis is that the business wins by designing how offers interact over time. A weak front end can still be useful if the rest of the model captures spend, speed, and continuity intelligently.
- **Usage Example(s):**
  - A company could break even on a starter offer, profit on the premium implementation, and build durable value through continuity and renewals rather than forcing the first sale to do everything.

## Frameworks
### A money model is the path that gets customers to spend more, faster, and more often
- **ID:** `100m-money-models-how-to-make-money-001`
- **Problem(s) Solved:**
  - Helps owners stop relying on a single offer and start designing how money flows across the customer journey.
- **How It Works:** The book defines money models as structured combinations of offers that increase what each customer is worth over time. Instead of one isolated sale, the business creates a sequence of entry, expansion, retention, and salvage offers.
- **Usage Example(s):**
  - A creator business could map a free lead magnet, a paid starter product, a premium implementation offer, and a continuity program as one money model instead of running each piece independently.
- **Source Notes:** Sections: Start Here; What’s A Money Model?
- **Related Items:** None

### The four offer types that power money models
- **ID:** `100m-money-models-how-to-make-money-002`
- **Problem(s) Solved:**
  - Helps operators organize monetization options into a manageable system rather than a random list of ideas.
- **How It Works:** The book structures money models around attraction offers, upsell offers, downsell offers, and continuity offers. Each type solves a different moment in the buyer journey.
- **Usage Example(s):**
  - A B2B consultancy might use a free audit as attraction, an implementation package as upsell, a paid workshop as downsell, and a monthly advisory retainer as continuity.
- **Source Notes:** Sections: The Four Types of Offers That Make Money Models
- **Related Items:** None
