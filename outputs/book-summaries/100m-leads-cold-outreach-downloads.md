## Tactical Ideas
### Start cold outreach with a low-friction question
- **ID:** `100m-leads-cold-outreach-downloads-001`
- **Problem(s) Solved:**
  - Helps cold outreach get replies from strangers by asking for engagement before asking for commitment.
- **How It Works:** The download pack repeatedly uses ultra-simple openers such as 'Are you still looking to...' so the first ask is easy to answer. The goal is to start a conversation, not to close in the first line.
- **Usage Example(s):**
  - A SaaS company could message prospects with 'Still looking to reduce churn in your membership base?' rather than a paragraph about its product and features.
- **Source Notes:** Sections: Cold Email & DM Scripts; Are you still looking to...
- **Related Items:** None

### Keep the first message plain text and frictionless
- **ID:** `100m-leads-cold-outreach-downloads-002`
- **Problem(s) Solved:**
  - Helps cold messages avoid looking like automated spam or obvious marketing collateral.
- **How It Works:** The pack explicitly recommends no images, no links, no frills, and often just a single question. Simple messages create curiosity and lower the resistance to replying.
- **Usage Example(s):**
  - A consultant can send a plain-text LinkedIn message with one clear question and hold the booking link for the follow-up after the prospect engages.
- **Source Notes:** Sections: Cold Email & DM Scripts
- **Related Items:** None

### Relate first, then transition to the problem
- **ID:** `100m-leads-cold-outreach-downloads-003`
- **Problem(s) Solved:**
  - Helps outreach feel human enough to answer while still moving quickly toward qualification.
- **How It Works:** The outreach scripts use small personalized observations to open, then pivot fast into the business problem being solved. Rapport is meant to reduce friction, not to become endless small talk.
- **Usage Example(s):**
  - A fitness coach might note a detail from a prospect's profile, then quickly ask whether work or family schedule makes training consistency difficult.
- **Source Notes:** Sections: Messenger Setter Script; Open; Relate; Transition
- **Related Items:** None

### Speed and open loops keep cold outreach alive
- **ID:** `100m-leads-cold-outreach-downloads-005`
- **Problem(s) Solved:**
  - Helps businesses avoid losing momentum between reply, booking, and actual conversation.
- **How It Works:** The scripts emphasize same-day movement, open loops around booking, and fast follow-up because time kills deals. Once the prospect responds, the process should compress rather than drag out.
- **Usage Example(s):**
  - If a lead says they will grab a time later, the setter can keep the chat open and follow up the same day instead of waiting several days and letting interest decay.
- **Source Notes:** Sections: Get On The Phone; Follow Up; TKAD Time Kills All Deals
- **Related Items:** None

## Frameworks
### Use pain, curiosity, then a live conversation
- **ID:** `100m-leads-cold-outreach-downloads-004`
- **Problem(s) Solved:**
  - Helps setters move from casual reply to real sales opportunity without over-typing the whole solution.
- **How It Works:** The sequence is acknowledge, clarify goal, label the problem, review past pain, create curiosity, and get on the phone. The scripts explicitly warn that typing the whole answer is slower and less effective than a short live call.
- **Usage Example(s):**
  - A marketing agency can ask what the prospect has tried, what results they wanted, and what keeps failing, then say it has ideas and suggest a quick call that day.
- **Source Notes:** Sections: Sales Conversation Begins; Clarify Goal; Label With Problem; Overview Pain; Create Curiosity; Get On The Phone
- **Related Items:** None
