# $100M Launch Affiliate Black Book

## Executive Snapshot
This is a campaign execution handbook for affiliates promoting the `$100M Money Models` launch. The most reusable ideas are not the exact templates but the mechanics underneath them: one core promise repeated across channels, live-only urgency, shared upside between referrer and registrant, a countdown cadence that intensifies as the event nears, and strict simplification rules for copy.

## Tactical Ideas
### Keep One Core Message Across Every Channel
The campaign recycles the same spine everywhere: free event, a major business benefit (`get customers to spend more money`), a secret project available only live, and milestone-based affiliate rewards.

### Escalate Urgency Through A Countdown Sequence
The email and SMS system moves from 21 days out to live-now messaging, tightening the time window while repeating the same benefit stack and the same action.

### Give The Audience A Reason To Use Your Link
The affiliate angle works because the registrant is not only helping the promoter. They also unlock shared bonuses, which makes the referral link feel cooperative rather than extractive.

### Translate The Same Offer Into Native Channel Formats
The handbook adapts one offer into Meta ads, email, SMS, friend texts, group posts, Slack posts, organic posts, and YouTube scripts. The lesson is that distribution breadth comes from formatting, not from inventing a new pitch per channel.

## Strategic Ideas
### Campaigns Convert Better When The Benefit Is Concrete And Eventized
The message is not vague personal development. It promises a specific business outcome, wraps it in a large live event, and adds exclusivity through a live-only reveal.

### Affiliate Promotion Works Better When It Feels Like Come With Me
Many of the templates frame the promotion as an invitation to attend together, not a pure affiliate push. That lowers resistance and makes the promoter feel like a guide rather than a solicitor.

### Shared Bonuses Turn Referral Behavior Into A Team Goal
Leaderboard prizes and milestone unlocks create a social reason to keep promoting after the first share. This is especially powerful in teams, groups, and communities where pooled signups feel collaborative.

## Frameworks
### Launch Message Stack
The basic stack is: `big event -> specific business upside -> secret or exclusive live-only asset -> free entry -> deadline -> register now`. The exact hook changes, but the persuasion spine stays stable.

### Countdown Promotion Framework
Use spaced reminders at 21 days, 14 days, 7 days, 4 days, 24 hours, and live-now. Each message should restate the event, the benefit, what is lost by missing live attendance, and the immediate next action.

### Hormozi Copy Rules
Write below third-grade reading level, prefer present tense and active voice, remove adverbs, shorten sentences, use positive phrasing, and cut redundant words. The point is to make copy feel punchy, effortless, and easy to forward.
