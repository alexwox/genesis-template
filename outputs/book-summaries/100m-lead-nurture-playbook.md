## Tactical Ideas
### Speed compounds across the whole follow-up chain
- **ID:** `100m-lead-nurture-playbook-004`
- **Problem(s) Solved:**
  - Helps businesses capture intent before it decays and before competitors respond.
- **How It Works:** Fast response is not just about the first reply; it also applies to confirmations, reschedules, reminder replies, and day-of issue handling. Delay lowers momentum and increases drop-off.
- **Usage Example(s):**
  - A med spa could route new leads to a rep within a minute, confirm the appointment quickly, and text back immediately when the prospect asks to move the slot rather than waiting until the next day.
- **Source Notes:** Sections: Pillar II Speed; Execution
- **Related Items:** None

### Personalized, repeated touches beat generic automation alone
- **ID:** `100m-lead-nurture-playbook-005`
- **Problem(s) Solved:**
  - Helps teams improve show rates by making follow-up feel relevant enough to answer instead of easy to ignore.
- **How It Works:** The playbook does not reject automation; it argues that automation works best when combined with personalization and enough total touches. Personalized reminders and human replies keep the lead moving.
- **Usage Example(s):**
  - A B2B agency can send an automated reminder sequence but still have the setter reference the prospect's stated goal, timing, or pain point in manual follow-ups to increase response and attendance.
- **Source Notes:** Sections: Pillar III Personalization; Pillar IV Volume; Lead Nurture Checklist
- **Related Items:** None

## Strategic Ideas
### If they do not show, they cannot buy
- **ID:** `100m-lead-nurture-playbook-001`
- **Problem(s) Solved:**
  - Helps businesses treat appointment attendance as a core revenue problem rather than a minor operational annoyance.
- **How It Works:** The playbook reframes nurture as a show-rate system. If scheduled leads do not respond, schedule, or attend, the sales team never gets a real chance to close.
- **Usage Example(s):**
  - An agency with plenty of booked calls but weak revenue could focus first on reminders, response rules, and appointment-confirmation workflows before rewriting the sales script.
- **Source Notes:** Sections: Lead Nurture
- **Related Items:** None

### Use data to find the real drivers of show rate
- **ID:** `100m-lead-nurture-playbook-003`
- **Problem(s) Solved:**
  - Helps teams improve attendance based on observed behavior rather than intuition.
- **How It Works:** The origin story of the playbook comes from mining a huge appointment dataset to identify the variables most correlated with people actually showing. The principle is to let real behavior shape the follow-up system.
- **Usage Example(s):**
  - A business that handles thousands of booked calls can compare show rates by reply time, reminder cadence, confirmation format, and appointment type to isolate the biggest levers.
- **Source Notes:** Sections: Lead Nurture origin story
- **Related Items:** None

## Frameworks
### The four pillars of lead nurture
- **ID:** `100m-lead-nurture-playbook-002`
- **Problem(s) Solved:**
  - Helps teams diagnose where nurture is failing instead of treating follow-up as one generic activity.
- **How It Works:** The book organizes better lead nurture around availability, speed, personalization, and volume. The pillars work together: being reachable, replying fast, sounding human, and following up enough times to matter.
- **Usage Example(s):**
  - A home-services company can audit whether it is losing deals because the rep only replies during office hours, takes too long, sends generic reminders, or gives up after two messages.
- **Source Notes:** Sections: Four Pillars of Lead Nurture; Pillar I Availability; Pillar II Speed; Pillar III Personalization; Pillar IV Volume
