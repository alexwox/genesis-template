# High-CAGR Market Discovery Skill

## Purpose
Find structurally durable, high-growth markets — not hype cycles. Combines macro structural analysis with micro bottleneck discovery to identify markets with sustainable 10–20 year tailwinds.

---

## The Core Filter (Use First)

Before any framework, a market must pass all four investor questions:

1. Is growth **structural** or cyclical?
2. Is demand **forced** or optional?
3. Is the problem becoming **more painful** over time?
4. Is **technology unlocking** new capability?

If yes to all four → high-CAGR candidate. If not → likely a hype cycle.

---

## Macro Frameworks

### Framework 1 — Structural Force Analysis
Identify forces that must push the market forward for 10–20 years regardless of sentiment.

**The four strongest structural forces:**

| Force | Examples | Markets Created |
|---|---|---|
| Demographics | aging populations, urbanization, migration | eldercare, longevity biotech, retirement services |
| Technology step changes | internet, cloud, AI | SaaS, vertical SaaS, AI tooling |
| Regulation | ESG, KYC/AML, cybersecurity compliance | compliance tech, regtech |
| Cost curve collapse | solar, genome sequencing, GPUs, cloud storage | new industries appear when costs drop 10-100x |

Rule: Structural forces create *guaranteed* demand. Demographics are the most reliable because people cannot stop aging.

---

### Framework 2 — Inevitable Problem Framework
Ask: "What problems become unavoidable as the world evolves?"

| Trend | Inevitable Problem |
|---|---|
| AI adoption | AI governance & safety |
| Remote work | identity / security |
| EV adoption | charging infrastructure |
| Data explosion | data management |
| Aging population | chronic care |

If the problem *must* be solved, the market grows.

---

### Framework 3 — Pain + Budget + Growth Filter
Simple but powerful. Market must satisfy all three:

1. **Pain** — people urgently want it solved
2. **Budget** — they already spend money on it
3. **Growth** — the number of buyers is increasing

| Market | Pain | Budget | Growth |
|---|---|---|---|
| Cybersecurity | huge | huge | exploding |
| Eldercare | huge | large | guaranteed |
| Climate risk analytics | rising | rising | fast |

---

### Framework 4 — Market Pull vs. Market Push Test
**Critical distinction.**

- **Push markets** — people must be convinced → often collapse (metaverse, NFTs, Web3 tools)
- **Pull markets** — people are desperate → sustainable growth (cybersecurity, cloud infra, fraud detection, payment infra)

High CAGR comes from pull, not push.

---

### Framework 5 — Second-Order Effect Framework
The biggest markets often appear in the second or third wave after a primary technology:

- First wave: internet
- Second wave: SaaS
- Third wave: dev tools / infrastructure
- Fourth wave: AI tooling

Ask: "What secondary layers does this primary technology create?"

## Micro / Industry Frameworks

### Framework 6 — Workflow Digitization Framework
Massive growth markets appear when manual industry workflows go digital.

| Industry | Digitization Market |
|---|---|
| Law | legal tech |
| Healthcare | health tech |
| Construction | contech |
| Insurance | insurtech |

Steps: Industry → Manual workflow → Software replaces it → Vertical SaaS

---

### Framework 7 — Adjacency Explosion Framework
Large technologies create ecosystems of secondary markets that often grow faster than the primary.

Example (AI):
- Primary market: model providers
- Secondary markets: vector databases, model monitoring, AI security, AI governance, prompt management, agent infrastructure

---

### Framework 8 — Bottleneck Market Framework
Constraints in large industries create concentrated growth. Everyone depends on the bottleneck, so it gets funded and scaled.

| Industry | Bottleneck |
|---|---|
| Semiconductors | lithography |
| Shipping | ports |
| Construction | permitting |
| Healthcare | staffing |

---

### Framework 9 — Global Inefficiency Transfer Framework
Large market appears when a new technology hits an inefficient incumbent industry.

| Inefficient Industry | Technology Transfer |
|---|---|
| Finance | fintech |
| Healthcare | health tech |
| Logistics | supply chain software |

Massive inefficiency + enabling technology = massive growth opportunity.

---

### Framework 10 — Total Addressable Friction Framework
Problems that occur millions of times per day. Even tiny improvements become billion-dollar markets.

| Activity | Friction |
|---|---|
| Payments | processing |
| Identity | authentication |
| Documents | signing |
| Support | ticket handling |

## The Discovery Loop (Macro → Micro)

Use this sequence to move from trend to specific opportunity:

```
Macro trend
↓
Structural force (which of the 4 forces is driving this?)
↓
Industry workflow (where does this force hit real work?)
↓
Bottleneck (what is the constraint within that workflow?)
↓
Offer pillar (what specific problem can be solved here?)
```

**Example:**
```
AI adoption
↓
Companies deploying models at scale
↓
Risk of hallucinations and failures
↓
No monitoring/observability tooling
↓
AI observability startups
```

## CAGR Benchmarks & Percentile Distribution

Use this to calibrate whether a market's growth rate is actually exceptional or just normal.

| Percentile | CAGR | Interpretation |
|---|---|---|
| 0.1% | -10% | Collapsing industry (fax machines, physical media) |
| 1% | -5% | Severe structural decline |
| 5% | -2% | Shrinking |
| 10% | 0% | Stagnant |
| 25% | 2% | Slow, GDP-like growth |
| 50% (median) | 5% | Average industry |
| 75% | 10% | Solid growth sector |
| 90% | 18% | Very strong growth |
| 95% | 25% | Elite growth market |
| 99% | 40% | Hypergrowth |
| 99.9% | 70–100%+ | New platform wave |

**How to interpret each zone:**

- **<0%** — Declining. Consolidation strategies only, not startups.
- **0–5%** — Mature (insurance, banking, construction, grocery). Win through efficiency, not growth.
- **5–10%** — Healthy but normal (logistics, healthcare services, edtech, accounting software). Great businesses possible but market isn't explosive.
- **10–20%** — Venture-scale sweet spot (cybersecurity, cloud infra, fintech, digital advertising). Multiple large companies can emerge simultaneously.
- **20–40%** — Rare and extremely attractive (smartphones 2007–2015, SaaS 2010–2020, e-commerce early 2000s, GPUs for AI).
- **40%+** — Early platform shift (internet ~60%, cloud early ~45%, generative AI ~60–100%). High upside but risk of saturation/collapse.

**The sweet spot for offer pillar hunting: 12–25% CAGR.**
- Growth is strong, budgets are expanding
- Competition hasn't saturated yet
- Market isn't purely hype

**Critical insight: CAGR alone is not enough.**

You want: high CAGR + large TAM + structural drivers + unsolved problems.

| Market | CAGR | Outcome |
|---|---|---|
| NFT marketplaces | 80% | Bubble — collapsed |
| Cybersecurity | 15% | Massive durable industry |
| Eldercare | 9% | Extremely durable |

Durability beats raw CAGR every time.

**The wind metaphor:**
- 0–5% CAGR → calm sea
- 5–10% → light breeze
- 10–20% → strong wind
- 20–40% → storm tailwind
- 40%+ → hurricane (exciting but dangerous)

The best founders sail where wind + stability coexist.

## Quick Heuristic for High-CAGR Markets

A market is likely to have durable high CAGR if it has:

- Structural driver (not sentiment)
- Increasing pain over time (not decreasing)
- New enabling technology (unlocks what was previously impossible)
- Fragmented incumbents (no single dominant player yet)
